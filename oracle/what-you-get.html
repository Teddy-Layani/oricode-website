What Oracle Does

A publication, not a broker.

What is Oracle?

Oracle is a market analysis publication. You subscribe to receive commentary and data about markets and the live trading system that powers this platform. Oracle is not a broker, advisor, or signal service.

What your subscription includes

  • Regime analysis — volatility, breadth, cross-asset flows, dollar regime, session context
  • Retrospective commentary — what drove performance in closed periods, with attribution
  • Methodology discussion — how the system thinks about inputs, gates, and risk at a conceptual level
  • Published performance — Sharpe, drawdown, profit factor, with sample sizes and confidence intervals
  • Symbol-level analytical context — framed as commentary, never as instruction

What Oracle will never do

  • Tell you what to buy, sell, or hold
  • Share current positions, fills, or exits of the live system
  • Set price targets, stops, or sizing for your account
  • Adjust output based on your holdings, risk profile, or account size
  • Guarantee future returns or forward performance
  • Provide tax, legal, or personalized financial guidance

Same question, same answer

Every subscriber asking the same topic in the same publication window receives the same response. No personalization, no ordering effects. This keeps Oracle a publication rather than individualized advice.

Example

What Oracle won't say
"Buy [symbol] at [price], target [price], stop [price]."
What Oracle will say
"The sector this name sits in has shown relative strength recently. Not investment advice."

Who Oracle is for

Oracle is available to Israeli residents only at this stage. US, UK, and EU access is restricted.

Oracle provides analytical data and commentary. It is not investment advice under the Regulation of Investment Advice Law (5755-1995). Past performance does not predict future results. Investing carries risk of loss.
